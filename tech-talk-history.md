| 2026-07-13 | Electrodermal activity (EDA / galvanic skin response) in wearables — Empatica EmbracePlus (research-grade), Fitbit Sense palm-plate spot-check, BIOPAC/Shimmer3 (lab), Samsung Galaxy Watch finger EDA, and Garmin Cirqa (mid-July 2026) | Sympathetic-arm-of-ANS specific signal that HRV cannot deliver because HRV integrates both parasympathetic and sympathetic tone. Eccrine sweat glands on glabrous skin (palm, fingertip, wrist) have no parasympathetic innervation, so skin-conductance rises track sympathetic activation on a 1–3 s latency. Tonic (SCL) + phasic (SCR) decomposition; Boucsein 2012 is the reference; SPR publication guidelines set the field standard. Consumer wrist-EDA is a hard engineering problem — motion artefact, sweat saturation and 5–10× individual variability in baseline SCL. Pegged to Garmin's Canadian CIPO (2483360), UK (UK00004405896) and EUIPO (019384825) trademark filings all surfacing by 8–10 July 2026 with "emotional stress" explicitly listed as a distinct sensor class; the5krunner narrows launch to 15 or 22 July 2026 based on FCC and UK pre-publication timers. If Cirqa delivers passive continuous wrist EDA in a $250–500 subscription-free wristband it is the first-in-category consumer product; validation in-the-wild will decide whether it becomes a genuine second layer on top of HRV or a marketing signal that rots in the field. |
| 2026-07-20 | Muscle-oxygenation wearables (near-infrared spectroscopy / NIRS) — Moxy Monitor (Fortiori Design), Train.Red FYER/PLUS (Artinis Medical Systems), NNOXX One (with nitric-oxide channel), Humon (assets → WHOOP 2020), plus the WHOOP US patent 12,594,037 B2 (7 April 2026, thigh/chest/arm placement, Humon founders as inventors) and Garmin "Muscle Battery" trademark race | Two narrow-band LEDs (~660 nm and ~855 nm) with a photodetector array at 5–50 mm reads SmO<sub>2</sub> % and tHb at the working muscle — the signal HRV, SpO<sub>2</sub> and RPE cannot supply because they read whole-body / vascular data. Continuous-wave NIRS is the field standard; frequency-domain and time-domain more accurate but lab-only. Perrey &amp; Ferrari 2023 (<em>Sports Medicine</em>) confirms NIRS as validated for skeletal-muscle O<sub>2</sub> supply/utilisation dynamics; SmO<sub>2</sub> desaturation slope during incremental exercise coincides with lactate-based thresholds (Feldmann et al. <em>EJAP</em> 2023). Weak spots: static values, inter-day repeatability of absolute SmO<sub>2</sub>, and 5–15 % unmodelled skinfold-thickness error. Cross-validation of a wearable CW-NIRS against gold-standard FD-NIRS in cyclists (<em>Bioengineering</em>, Nov 2025) found strong agreement early but growing bias at high intensity. Not pegged to a single launch — pegged to the category becoming the next competitive front in wearables: WHOOP's April 2026 NIRS patent + Humon acqui-hire; Garmin's "Muscle Battery" trademark and analyst read-through pointing to an SmO<sub>2</sub> module in 2026–2027; NNOXX shipping the only current consumer NIRS with a serious app layer; category CAGR pegged 14.8 % through 2035. Practitioner-side: Moxy / Train.Red on thigh belly is the current standard for threshold and interval-quality work; treat any 2026 consumer-wrist NIRS claim skeptically until independent per-device validation drops. |
