# Sport Tech Weekly

An automated weekly newsletter on sport technology — vendor releases, new sensors, league adoption, recent literature, upcoming events, and a deep-dive into one sensor each issue. Aimed at performance practitioners and sport scientists who want to stay at the forefront of the field without doing the weekly research themselves.

**Read the latest:** https://petterjolstad.github.io/sport-tech-newsletter/

## What's in each issue

- **Big stories** — the most consequential vendor releases and adoption stories from the past 7 days. International scope. Mix of established players and emerging up-and-coming products (clearly flagged 🌱).
- **Recent literature** — 3–5 highlighted peer-reviewed papers from the past week, with practical-relevance summaries, plus an extended "other notable papers" list.
- **Upcoming events** — relevant conferences, vendor user-groups, and webinars in the next four weeks.
- **Podcast pick** — one episode from the past week worth your time.
- **Tech-talk deep-dive** — a ~400-word feature on one sensor: what it is, leading providers, specs that matter for practitioners, validation status, and what's new. A 12-month cooldown keeps topics from repeating (except for major new releases).

## How it works

The newsletter is generated every **Monday at 09:00 Europe/Oslo** by a [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) routine running in Anthropic's cloud. Each run:

1. Searches the past 7 days across vendor news, sport-tech media, peer-reviewed journals, preprint servers, and industry podcasts.
2. Reads `tech-talk-history.md` to pick a fresh tech-talk subject under the 12-month cooldown rule.
3. Fetches relevant images (`og:image`) from each story's source URL — no stock photos, no placeholders.
4. Generates a self-contained HTML file styled in a warm editorial layout.
5. Commits the new edition, updates `index.html`, and appends to `tech-talk-history.md`.

Each issue is a standalone HTML file at the repo root, named `YYYY-MM-DD.html`. `index.html` is the rolling landing page.

## Editorial principles

- **No fabrication.** If a section has no genuine news from the past 7 days, the newsletter says so plainly rather than padding.
- **Sources for every claim.** Every product, study, or event mentioned has a verifiable source link.
- **Practitioner over marketing.** Takeaways are framed as "this changes X because Y", not as vendor copy.
- **Honest about maturity.** Emerging tech is flagged with its development stage (prototype / pilot / commercially available) and reasonable skepticism about what's unvalidated.
